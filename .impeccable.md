## Design Context

### Users

Your Mascot Now is a public character-recipe library for designers, developers, founders, and content creators who want expressive black-and-white mascot artwork built around their own identity. Visitors should be able to quickly browse the first 80 scenes, search and filter the collection, copy a scene-specific prompt, use their own portrait as the identity reference in ChatGPT Images, inspect an individual piece, share its URL, and download the supplied original or SVG.

### Brand Personality

Tactile, handmade, clear, and precise. The interface should feel like a real illustrator's desk and character-recipe cabinet: approachable enough to explore, disciplined enough to trust, and explicit about the path from photo to personalized mascot.

### Aesthetic Direction

Use restrained skeuomorphism grounded in illustration work: literal white paper, a neutral silver-grey work surface, taped proof prints, recessed search fields, raised switches, physical ink swatches, paper recipe cards, and soft directional shadows. The interface palette is strictly achromatic—white, light grey, graphite, and charcoal in light mode; near-black, graphite, soft grey, and off-white in dark mode. Only a user-selected mascot ink may introduce color. Corners should feel handled rather than geometric, with small-to-medium radii and believable bevels instead of pills. Provide equally intentional light and dark studio modes; the mascot ink and artwork surface should reverse with the theme. Let users select any custom ink color, automatically pair it with a contrasting surface, and carry that choice into prompts and SVG exports. Avoid beige or blue UI accents, brutalist hard rules, flat contact sheets, glossy 2000s chrome, oversized display typography, cream editorial layouts, dark-neon technology aesthetics, glass effects, and gradient text. Typography should stay compact and legible while materials provide the character.

### Design Principles

1. Treat catalog structure as useful information: every number, mark, and label should identify an illustration or category.
2. Keep the artwork dominant and use the "Photo → mascot" recipe strip as the signature system.
3. Make searching, filtering, prompt copying, theme and color switching, previewing, sharing, and downloading work without explanation.
4. Use motion for orientation and state changes, with an equivalent reduced-motion experience.
5. Preserve accessibility, responsive behavior, and image performance as part of the visual quality floor.
